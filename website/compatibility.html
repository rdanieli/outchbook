Compatibility and behavior notes

OuchBook is built around Apple Silicon MacBook hardware behavior. It uses real sensor and lid-close data, so model differences matter.

Best current target

Apple Silicon MacBooks on macOS 13 or newer.

Current non-targets

Intel Macs, desktop Macs, and any machine where the expected lid or motion sensor path is unavailable.

What the app measures

OuchBook uses lid motion and hardware sensor data to estimate close intensity. It is not just a timer that plays a sound after sleep.

Why clips can be cut

Full lid closure causes forced sleep on macOS. Once the machine reaches that point, audio can be interrupted abruptly.

What to expect in practice

Works well

  • Pre-close reactions that start while the lid is still moving
  • Short clips with fast attack
  • Tier changes driven by how fast you close the lid

Not realistic on macOS

  • Letting a full long scream continue after the lid is fully shut
  • Relying on post-sleep playback
  • Guaranteeing uninterrupted audio after forced sleep begins
OuchBook is intentionally honest: the joke is engineered around the time window before full closure, because that is the part macOS still allows the app to win.
Why not keep the Mac awake after the lid closes?

That would turn OuchBook into a sleep-management utility instead of a simple menu bar gag app. The first release avoids global power-management tricks.

Will support expand to more Mac models later?

Possibly. The app is structured so hardware-specific integrations can be adapted over time as more machines are tested.
