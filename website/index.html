How OuchBook works

OuchBook watches the closing motion of your MacBook lid, estimates how intense the close was, and picks a matching scream profile before sleep fully takes over.

Tier 1: Gentle

Small close, low drama. OuchBook answers with a soft, brief ow... instead of a full meltdown.

Tier 2: Normal

Confident close, sharp reaction. This is the quick, punchy OW! lane.

Tier 3: Rage close

Fast slam energy. OuchBook randomly rotates between agony, distorted WHY, soul-leaving chaos, and not again for more personality.

What makes it funny

The app does not play the same dramatic clip every time. Tier 3 picks from multiple alternate sounds, so hard closes feel a little unpredictable instead of repetitive.

Designed for reality, not fantasy

macOS will interrupt audio when the lid is fully closed. OuchBook is tuned as a pre-close gag: it starts the sound early so you hear the punch before the system wins.

Compatibility and current limitations

OuchBook is intentionally narrow in scope for the first public release. It is built around Apple Silicon hardware behavior and direct distribution.

Best fit today

Apple Silicon MacBooks running macOS 13 or later.

Not a perfect fit

Intel Macs, desktops, and any machine where the expected lid or motion sensor path is absent.

Important caveat

If you close the lid all the way, macOS may cut the sound off. The product is tuned to play before full closure, not after the machine is already asleep.

FAQ

Does OuchBook keep screaming after the lid is fully closed?

No. Once the Mac reaches forced sleep, audio can be cut abruptly. OuchBook is designed to trigger before the final close, which is the only practical way to make the joke audible.

Why direct download instead of the Mac App Store?

The app depends on lower-level hardware access patterns that are much better suited to direct distribution and rapid iteration.

How do licenses work right now?

Purchases are handled through PayPal and licenses are delivered manually for the first release. That keeps the launch simple while the product is still being tuned across Mac models.

Will it work on every MacBook?

No. Hardware behavior varies. OuchBook is currently focused on supported Apple Silicon MacBooks, and compatibility notes are published separately.
